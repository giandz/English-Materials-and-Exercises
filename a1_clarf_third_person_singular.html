He, she, and it. Titles for people. Verbs with -s.

Note: we use these titles with a family name. We say Mr. Smith. We don't say Mr. John. And remember: Mrs. Lopez = she.
grammar: he, she, it
●  noun ●  pronoun ●  negative ●  question
he / she / it
✓ Positive
Mr. Smith he
works in the city. (work → works)
My bike it
needs new tires. (need → needs)
Your sister she
studies a lot. (study → studies)
✗ Negative
Mrs. Lopez she
doesn't drink coffee. (do → does)
The store it
doesn't open on Sundays. (do → does)
? Question
Does
your brother he
play soccer? (do → does)
Does
the bus it
stop here? (do → does)
Remember: the verb has -s only in the positive sentence. We use doesn't and does for negative and question. The main verb has no -s: ✗ Does he plays? → ✓ Does he play?
