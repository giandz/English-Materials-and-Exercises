Giving Directions

Two short conversations using common direction phrases.

1. A tourist asks for directions
🗺️ tourist and a local, on the street
🧳
Tourist
Excuse me. How do I get to the museum?
🧑
Local
Sure! Go straight for two blocks.
🧳
Tourist
Okay, straight for two blocks. Then what?
🧑
Local
Then turn left at the park. Walk along the river for a little while.
🧳
Tourist
Turn left, then walk along the river. Got it.
🧑
Local
Right. You will see a bridge. Cross the bridge.
🧳
Tourist
Cross the bridge. Is the museum near there?
🧑
Local
Almost. After the bridge, turn right. Go past the small church.
🧳
Tourist
Turn right, go past the church. And then?
🧑
Local
The museum is on the corner. It's opposite a big hotel.
🧳
Tourist
On the corner, opposite a hotel. Thank you so much!
🧑
Local
You're welcome. Have a nice day!
2. A delivery driver calls the recipient
📞 driver, lost, calls the customer
🚚
Driver
Hi! I have your package, but I can't find your house.
🏠
Customer
Okay, no problem! Where are you now?
🚚
Driver
I'm at the gas station on Elm Street.
🏠
Customer
Good. Go straight and go through the small tunnel.
🚚
Driver
Go straight, then through the tunnel. Okay.
🏠
Customer
After the tunnel, turn right. My street is between two big shops.
🚚
Driver
Turn right, between two shops. I see it!
🏠
Customer
Great. My house is next to a yellow house.
🚚
Driver
Next to a yellow house. Is it on the left or the right?
🏠
Customer
It's on the right. Blue door, number 12.
🚚
Driver
Hmm, I don't see it. Wait — I think I went too far.
🏠
Customer
No problem. Turn around and go back a little.
🚚
Driver
Turning around now... Oh, I see it! It's around the corner.
🏠
Customer
Yes! That's my house. Thank you!
Quick reference
Direction words are usually simple imperatives (turn, go, cross) followed by a direction or place. Listen for landmarks like a park, a bridge, or a shop — they help you know when to turn.
