Likes & Preferences

How much do you like it? From love to hate.

the scale
❤️
I love...
I love mushroom pizza.
🤩
I really like... / I'm crazy about... / I'm really into...
I really like salsa music.
👍
I like... / I'm into...
I like playing soccer.
🙂

I'm OK with pineapple pizza.
😐

I don't mind rainy days.
🫤

I'm not a fan of spicy food.
👎

I really don't like horror films.
😖
I really don't like...
I really don't like horror films.
💀

I can't stand loud music.
compound nouns
A compound noun is two words that go together as one thing: mushroom + pizza = mushroom pizza.
salsa music mushroom pizza action movies horror films chocolate cake rainy days video games rock music
gerunds (verb + -ing)
After like / love / hate, a verb often takes -ing: play → playing.
playing soccer going for walks watching TV cooking dinner reading books dancing swimming traveling
grammar: sentence patterns
like / love / hate + noun or gerund
+ noun (or compound noun)
I love mushroom pizza.
She likes salsa music.
+ gerund (verb + -ing)
I like playing soccer.
He hates waking up early.
negative
I don't like horror films.
She doesn't like cooking.
Remember: with he / she / it, the verb gets -s: likes, loves, hates. The word after stays the same (noun or -ing form).
reference: prefer
Prefer compares two or more things. We say prefer 🫴🟢1 (to 🫳🟢2 or 🫳🟢3).
🍕 🟩🟩◼️◼️◼️
🍔 🟩🟩🟩🟩◼️
🍝 🟩🟩◼️◼️◼️
I prefer hamburgers to pizza or pasta.
🎬 🟩🟩🟩◼️◼️
📺 🟩🟩🟩🟩🟩
I prefer watching TV shows to watching movies.
Note: after prefer, use a noun or a gerund (-ing) — not than. ✗ I prefer tea than coffee. → ✓ I prefer tea to coffee.
