grammar reference
have + object + past participle
have + object + past participle
More formal. Someone does something for you — you arranged it.

I had the sofa delivered.
She had her windows cleaned.
get + object + past participle
get + object + past participle
More informal. Similar meaning — you organised someone to do something.

I got the kettle fixed.
She got her shelves installed.
Key difference: have something done = neutral, often planned  ·  get something done = more effort or difficulty involved, or more informal register.
Both mean you did not do it yourself — someone did it for you.
