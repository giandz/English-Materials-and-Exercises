Grammar focus on the three pronunciations of the third person singular S ending: /s/, /z/, and /ɪz/, with rules, examples, and a sorting exercise.

/s/
After voiceless consonant sounds: /p/, /t/, /k/, /f/
workworks/wɝːks/He works at home.
eateats/iːts/She eats breakfast at eight.
drinkdrinks/drɪŋks/He drinks coffee.
walkwalks/wɔːks/She walks to school.
talktalks/tɔːks/He talks to his friends.
/z/
After voiced consonant sounds and vowel sounds: /b/, /d/, /g/, /v/, /m/, /n/, /l/, /r/ and all vowels
drivedrives/draɪvz/She drives to work.
livelives/lɪvz/He lives in the city.
comecomes/kʌmz/She comes home at five.
gogoes/ɡoʊz/He goes to school.
paypays/peɪz/She pays for lunch.
playplays/pleɪz/He plays with his dog.
/ɪz/
After sibilant sounds: /s/, /z/, /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /tʃ/, /dʒ/ — and always spelled -es
teachteaches/ˈtiːtʃɪz/She teaches English.
watchwatches/ˈwɒtʃɪz/He watches TV.
finishfinishes/ˈfɪnɪʃɪz/She finishes at six.
useuses/ˈjuːzɪz/He uses the bus.
washwashes/ˈwɒʃɪz/She washes the dishes.
