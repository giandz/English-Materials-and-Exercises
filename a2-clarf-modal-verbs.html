Modal verbs

can  ·  could  ·  should  ·  may  ·  might  ·  would

grammar reference
subject  +  modal  +  base verb   (no -s · no -ed · no to)
can ability  ·  possibility  ·  informal permission
She can drive a car.
She can't drive a bus.
He can speak English.
He can't speak French.
Can I sit here?
You can't park here.
could past ability  ·  polite requests  ·  weaker possibility
She could swim at age five.
He couldn't walk yet.
Could you open the door, please?
I couldn't do that, sorry.
It could be at the bus stop.
That couldn't be right.
should advice  ·  recommendation
You should eat breakfast.
You shouldn't skip meals.
She should see a doctor.
He shouldn't work so late.
You should go to bed early.
You shouldn't watch TV all day.
may formal permission  ·  possibility (50 %)
May I open the window?
You may not leave early.
It may rain today.
It may not be open.
She may come to the party.
He may not have time.
might weaker possibility (30 %)  ·  tentative suggestion
It might snow tomorrow.
It might not be cold.
She might come to class.
He might not finish on time.
You might like the coffee shop.
I might not have time today.
would polite requests  ·  offers  ·  preferences
Would you like some coffee?
I wouldn't like sugar, thanks.
I'd like a window seat.
She wouldn't like that.
Would you help me, please?
I wouldn't do that.
