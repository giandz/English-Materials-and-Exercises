Are you free this weekend?

A conversation using yes/no questions with "be" and other verbs.

💬 two classmates, Friday afternoon
●  question ●  negative ●  positive
Conversation
👨‍🦲
Jae
Are you free this weekend?
👩‍🦱
Lily
No, I'm not. I have a family lunch on Saturday.
👨‍🦲
Jae
Ah, okay. Does your family live nearby?
👩‍🦱
Lily
No, they don't. They live two hours away, so I need to drive there.
👨‍🦲
Jae
That's far! Are you tired of the drive?
👩‍🦱
Lily
Yes, I am. But it's worth it — I love seeing my grandparents.
👨‍🦲
Jae
That's nice. Does your grandmother cook?
👩‍🦱
Lily
Yes, she does. She makes the best soup in the world.
👨‍🦲
Jae
Sounds great. Well, what about Sunday? Are you busy then too?
👩‍🦱
Lily
No, I'm not. Sunday is free. Do you want to study together?
👨‍🦲
Jae
Yes, I do! Let's meet at the library at 3pm.
👩‍🦱
Lily
Perfect. See you then!
Quick reference
A short answer alone can sound abrupt. Adding extra information after a negative answer makes it more natural and polite: "No, I'm not. I have a family lunch on Saturday."
