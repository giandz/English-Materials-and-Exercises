We were so different!

Ben and Ana talk about their childhood habits.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑☕ old friends, catching up over coffee
●  question ●  negative ●  positive
Conversation
👧🎾❓
🧔‍♂️
Ben
Did you use to play any sports as a kid?
👧⚽
👩‍🦰
Ana
Yes! I used to play soccer every weekend.
🧒🎮
🧔‍♂️
Ben
Not me. I didn't use to like sports at all. I would stay home and play video games instead.
🧒📚❓
👩‍🦰
Ana
Sounds like you! Did you use to read a lot too?
🧒📖
🧔‍♂️
Ben
Yes, I used to read comic books all the time. And every night, my dad would read me a story.
👶😭
👩‍🦰
Ana
That's so sweet. I used to cry a lot at night as a baby, apparently — my mom says I never slept!
👧🛹❓

Ben
What about as a teenager? Did you use to skateboard or anything like that?
👧❌🛹

Ana
No, I never used to skateboard, but my brother would practice for hours in front of the house.
🧑🎸
🧔‍♂️
Ben
I used to be in a band, actually. We would practice every Friday in my garage.
👩‍🦰
Ana
No way! And now look at us — coffee and grown-up jobs.
🧑👧⏳
🧔‍♂️
Ben
True. We used to have so much free time — now it's all meetings!
Quick reference
Both used to and would describe repeated past habits, but only used to works with states like like or be. For actions, either works: My dad would read me a story = My dad used to read me a story.
