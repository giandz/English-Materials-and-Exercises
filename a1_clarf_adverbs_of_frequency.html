Adverbs of Frequency

How often something happens, from 100% of the time to 0%.

The scale

always almost always often / usually sometimes hardly ever / rarely almost never never
Word order: adverbs of frequency go before the main verb (She often cooks), but after the verb "be" (She is often tired) and after auxiliary verbs (She has always liked coffee).
The seven adverbs
100%
always

I always brush my teeth before bed.

95%
almost always

He's almost always the first one in the office.

75%
often / usually

We usually eat dinner together.

50%
sometimes

Sometimes I take the bus instead of driving.

25%
hardly ever / rarely

She rarely stays up past midnight.

5%
almost never

They almost never argue about money.

0%
never

I never eat breakfast before 8am.
