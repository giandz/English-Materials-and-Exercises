How often do you...?

A short conversation using everyday vocabulary and adverbs of frequency.

☕ two coworkers, Monday morning, coffee break
Conversation
🙂
Sam
Morning! Did you have a good weekend?
🙋
Ana
Pretty good. I usually just stay home on Saturdays, but this time we went hiking.
🙂
Sam
Nice! Do you go hiking a lot?
🙋
Ana
Not really. I rarely have time during the week, so weekends are my only chance.
🙂
Sam
Same here. I never have energy after work. I just cook dinner and go to bed early.
🙋
Ana
Do you cook every night?
🙂
Sam
Almost always. I sometimes order food, but only on Fridays.
🙋
Ana
That's smart. I often forget to eat lunch, so by dinner I'm starving anyway.
🙂
Sam
You should set an alarm! I always set one for lunch, or I'd forget too.
🙋
Ana
Good idea. I'll try that tomorrow.
Quick reference
Adverbs of frequency usually go before the main verb (I always eat) but after the verb "be" (I am always tired).
never — 0% rarely sometimes often usually always — 100%
