Past Perfect

The "past before the past" — showing what happened earlier, from a later point in the past.

Form: had + past participle. It's always relative to another past point — past perfect never stands alone.
1. One past action before another
The earlier event gets had + participle; the later one stays past simple.
When I arrived at the station, the train had already left.
🚆💨 had left
(1:00 pm)
→
🏃‍♀️ arrived
(1:10 pm)
→
📍 now
2. Explaining or giving a reason
Past perfect explains why something happened.
She was tired yesterday because she hadn't slept well the night before.
🌙💤❌ hadn't slept
(2 days ago)
→
😩 was tired
(yesterday)
→
📍 now
3. First time / "ever" up to a point in the past
Talking about an experience (or lack of one) up to a certain past moment.
That pasta was the best meal she had ever eaten.
🥘⭐❌🌮⭐❌ had eaten
(life so far)
→
🍝⭐ ate
(later)
→
📍 now
He had never seen the beach up until that day.
🏖️👀❌ had never seen
(life so far)
→
🏖️👀‼️ saw
(that day)
→
📍 now
4. Reported speech (backshift)
When reporting what someone said, past simple / present perfect shift back to past perfect.
👨‍💻"I finished the report." → He said he had finished the report.
Time expressions
These words often signal past perfect because they point to "before" another past moment:
already just never ever before after by the time by then when (up) until that day
Examples with time expressions
🍕 Had you ever tried sushi before that trip?
🎬 By the time we got to the cinema, the film had already started.
📦 She had just finished unpacking when the phone rang 📞.
🚪 He had never seen snow until that day ❄️.
