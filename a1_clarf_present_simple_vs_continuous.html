Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Two ways to talk about the present — routines and habits vs. actions happening right now.

Present Simple = things that happen again and again, or facts. Present Continuous = things happening right now, at this moment.
Forms
Present Simple
I work. She works. They don't work.
✓ Affirmative
I / You / We / They work.
He / She / It works.
✗ Negative
I / You / We / They don't work.
He / She / It doesn't work.
? Question
Do I / you / we / they work?
Does he / she / it work?
Present Continuous
I am working. She is working. They aren't working.
✓ Affirmative
I am working.
He / She / It is working.
You / We / They are working.
✗ Negative
I am not working.
He / She / It isn't working.
You / We / They aren't working.
? Question
Am I working?
Is he / she / it working?
Are you / we / they working?
When do we use each one?
UsePresent SimplePresent Continuous
Routine I brush my teeth every morning. —
Now — I am brushing my teeth right now.
Fact Water boils at 100°C. —
Temporary — I am staying with my sister this week.
Time expressions

Present Simple often uses:

always usually sometimes never every day on Mondays

Present Continuous often uses:

now right now at the moment today this week Look!
Examples together
I usually walk to work, but today I'm taking the bus because it's raining.
She doesn't like coffee, but right now she's drinking a cup because she's tired.
