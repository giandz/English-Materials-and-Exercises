A1 · Beginner

What do you like?

Mia and Tom compare their tastes in music, movies, and food.

☕ two friends, coffee shop
●  likes / preferences ●  agreeing ●  disagreeing
Conversation
👩
Mia
What music do you prefer, Tom? Rock or salsa?
🧑
Tom
I prefer rock to salsa music. What about you?
👩
Mia
So do I! I really like rock too.
🧑
Tom
Nice! Do you like watching movies?
👩
Mia
Yes, I do. I love action movies. I don't like horror films, though.
🧑
Tom
Me too! I can't stand horror films.
👩
Mia
And videogames? Do you like any? I'm not a big fan of them.
🧑
Tom
Neither do I. I'm not intovideogames.
👩
Mia
What about food? I'm crazy about Chinese food.
🧑
Tom
Really? I don't mind Chinese food, but I prefer Italian food to Chinese food.
👩
Mia
Oh, I don't. I really don't like Italian food.
🧑
Tom
Interesting! What about sports? I like playing soccer.
👩
Mia
I'm OK with soccer, but I prefer swimming to soccer.
🧑
Tom
Really? I don't. I hate swimming — the water is always too cold!
👩
Mia
That's funny! I thought you were a swimming fan.
🧑
Tom
I love many things, but swimming is not one of them.
Quick reference
To agree with a positive sentence, use So do I or Me too. To agree with a negative sentence, use Neither do I or Me neither. To disagree, say Really?, Oh, I don't, or I don't agree.
