B1 · Intermediate

I saw you yesterday!

Ana ran into Ben at the station and asks about his busy day.

🚉 train station, evening
●  past simple ●  past continuous ●  past perfect
Conversation
👩‍🦰
Ana
Hey Ben! I saw you yesterday at the coffee shop, but you were talking on the phone, so I didn't want to interrupt.
🧔‍♂️
Ben
Oh, that was you! Sorry, yesterday was such a busy day. By the time I got to the coffee shop, I had already missed two meetings.
👩‍🦰
Ana
Oh no! What happened?
🧔‍♂️
Ben
Well, while I was driving to the office, my car broke down. I hadn't checked the oil in months, so I wasn't surprised.
👩‍🦰
Ana
That's rough. So did you walk the rest of the way?
🧔‍♂️
Ben
Yes. And while I was walking, it started raining. Of course, I had left my umbrella at home.
👩‍🦰
Ana
😂 Poor you! So when you finally arrived at the coffee shop, what were you doing on the phone?
🧔‍♂️
Ben
I was explaining everything to my boss. She wasn't happy, because I had already rescheduled that meeting once before.
👩‍🦰
Ana
Wow. Did things get better after that?
🧔‍♂️
Ben
A little. By the time I got home, I had already forgotten about the whole thing. I just made dinner and went to bed early.
👩‍🦰
Ana
Well, I hope today is calmer! Was your car still broken down when you left for work this morning?
🧔‍♂️
Ben
No, luckily. The mechanic had already fixed it by the time I woke up. Today was much better.
Quick reference
Past simple tells the main events in order. Past continuous sets the scene or shows an action in progress when something else happened. Past perfect shows what happened before another past event — usually with words like already, by the time...
