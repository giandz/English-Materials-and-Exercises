A2 · Elementary

Used to / Would — Past Habits

Talking about things we did regularly in the past, but don't do anymore.

●  question ●  negative ●  positive
Used to + base verb talks about past habits, routines, and states that are no longer true. Would + base verb also talks about repeated past actions — but only actions, not states like "be", "have", or "like".
One person, different ages
Baby 👶 😴🍼
Child 🧒 🌳🚲
Teenager 🧑 🎮🫣
Adult (now) 🧑‍💼 ☕💻
As a baby 👶, I used to sleep all day 😴 and drink milk out of a baby bottle 🍼. As a child 🧒, I used to climb trees 🌳 and I would ride my bike 🚲 every afternoon. I used to be very shy 🫣 as a teenager 🧑, so I would play videogames at home 🎮, but I became more confident when I went to college.
Used to — all forms
used to actions & states
✓ Affirmative
👶 → 🧒 I used to be scared of the dark.
🧒 → 🧑 She used to play the piano.
✗ Negative
I didn't use to like vegetables.
They didn't use to live here.
? Question
Did you use to play sports?
Did he use to have long hair?
Would — affirmative only
would actions only
✓ Affirmative
🧒 Every summer, we would visit my grandparents 👵👴.
🧑 On weekends, he would skateboard 🛹 for hours.
✗ Negative / ? Question — uncommon
We rarely use wouldn't or would...? for past habits — use used to instead: didn't use to / did...use to.
✗ Not for states
✗ I would be shy. → ✓ I used to be shy 😳 (be = state)
✗ I would have a dog. → ✓ I used to have a dog 🐶 (have = state)
Side by side
👶🧒 I used to be afraid of dogs 🐶, but now I love them.
🧒🧑 Every Friday, my dad would take us for ice cream 🍦.
Did you use to live in another city 🏙️?
I didn't use to drink coffee ☕ — now I drink it every day!
