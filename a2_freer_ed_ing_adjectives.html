exercise 2 — discussion questions
Talk about these questions with a partner. Try to use -ed and -ing adjectives in your answers.
01
boring / bored
What kind of films, books, or TV shows do you find boring? When was the last time you were really bored?
e.g. I find very long historical films boring. I was bored last Sunday — I couldn't find anything to watch!
02
exciting / excited
What is something exciting that is going to happen in your life soon? How does it make you feel?
e.g. I'm going on holiday next month. I'm so excited — it's going to be an exciting trip!
03
surprising / surprised
What is the most surprising thing that has happened to you recently? Were you completely surprised, or did you expect it?
e.g. My colleague got promoted — it was very surprising. I was really surprised because she had only been here six months.
04
annoying / annoyed
What everyday things do you find most annoying? Think about transport, technology, or other people's habits.
e.g. I find it really annoying when the Wi-Fi is slow. I get annoyed when people don't reply to messages.
05
disappointing / disappointed
Have you ever been disappointed by a film, book, restaurant, or holiday that you had high expectations for?
e.g. The last restaurant I tried was really disappointing. I was so disappointed — the food looked amazing online!
06
interesting / interested
What topics do you find most interesting? Is there something new you have recently become interested in?
e.g. I find astronomy really interesting. I've recently become interested in learning more about space.
07
embarrassing / embarrassed
Can you describe an embarrassing moment? How did you feel? Do you still feel embarrassed about it now?
e.g. Once I called my teacher "mum" by accident. It was so embarrassing — I was embarrassed for the rest of the day!
08
satisfying / satisfied
What activities do you find most satisfying? When do you feel most satisfied at the end of a day?
e.g. I find cooking satisfying. I feel satisfied when I finish a big project at work.
Useful phrases for discussion
I find it really …ing I feel very …ed when It's so …ing that I was really …ed by I'm …ed in … What I find …ing is To be honest, I'm not very …ed by It was the most …ing thing
