Present Perfect Tense

formation
subject+ have / has+ past participle
Use have with I, you, we, they  ·  Use has with he, she, it
affirmative · negative · questions
affirmativesubject + have/has + past participle
I have visited Paris.
She has sent ten emails this week.
We have finished the homework.
He has worked here for two years.
negativesubject + have/has + not + past participle
I haven't seen that film yet.
She hasn't replied to my message.
They haven't eaten lunch today.
He hasn't called me this month.
questionshave/has + subject + past participle?
Have you used a computer today?
Has she finished her report?
Have they ever travelled abroad?
Short answers: Yes, I have. / No, she hasn't.
ever · never · yet · already · just
ever
in questions — at any time
Have you ever sent a voice message?
never
negative — not at any time
I have never used a smartwatch.
yet
negatives and questions — up to now
I haven't updated my phone yet.
Have you finished yet?
already
affirmatives — sooner than expected
She has already downloaded the app.
just
affirmatives — a moment ago
I have just received your email.
time expressions
today this week this month this year recently lately so far up to now since 2020 for two years in my life before
