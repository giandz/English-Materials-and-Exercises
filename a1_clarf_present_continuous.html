Use the present continuous to talk about something happening right now or around this time.
Form:
am / is / are + verb + ing
affirmative
I 'm working today.
She is cooking pasta.
They are watching TV.
negative
I 'm not working today.
She isn't cooking pasta.
They aren't watching TV.
question
Am I working today?
Is she cooking pasta?
Are they watching TV?

Three uses of the present continuous

The present continuous is not just for activities — it also describes people's clothes and the weather.

🏃
Use 1 — activities now
What is a person doing?
affirmative
negative
She is cooking dinner.
She isn't watching TV.
They are studying in the library.
They aren't playing outside.
He is talking on the phone.
He isn't sleeping.
